## Abstract
Understanding the causal logic of gene regulation typically relies on multi-modal paired data (such as single-cell RNA sequencing and ATAC sequencing). However, paired data is scarce, has significant batch effects, and is expensive to collect. This paper presents GeneMamba, a pure endogenous, bidirectional large-parameter Mamba2 architecture generative model. GeneMamba completely unsupervisedly decouples two latent variables with clear biological significance from the single-cell transcriptome expression matrix: continuous chromatin latent state Z (controlling expression amplitude) and binary transcription switch S (controlling expression presence/absence). The model enforces an irreversible causal chain Z→S→X, and naturally blocks all backdoor paths through structural design, making the observation distribution equivalent to the intervention distribution, thus endowing it with zero-shot counterfactual perturbation prediction capability. The entire training process does not rely on any ATAC-seq or other multi-modal data; ATAC is only used as an ex post zero-shot validation benchmark. Experiments show that GeneMamba not only accurately reconstructs the expression matrix, but also spontaneously recovers chromatin accessibility and transcription switch states under zero-shot conditions, verifying its strong endogenous causal discovery capability.

## 1. Introduction
Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) provides unprecedented resolution for depicting cellular heterogeneity and gene regulatory networks. However, transcriptome data is essentially the terminal output of gene regulation, and it is extremely challenging to reversely decouple upstream regulatory signals (such as chromatin state, transcription factor switches) from it. Existing methods either rely on paired ATAC-seq data as supervision signals, or use black-box deep generative models, lacking interpretable causal structures.

This paper presents GeneMamba, a structural causal state space model, whose core hypothesis is: Single-cell transcriptome data inherently contains sufficient statistics to separate chromatin amplitude and transcription switches. Based on this hypothesis, GeneMamba's design follows three iron rules:
- **Pure endogenous training**: The loss function only contains self-supervised terms, no external labels required.
- **Strict causal lock**: Structure hardcodes Z→S→X, ensuring biological logic is irreversible.
- **Bidirectional large parameter capacity**: Adopts forward and reverse dual Mamba2 streams to capture genome-wide bidirectional regulatory dependencies with linear complexity.

At the theoretical level, we prove that the model's structural causal graph naturally satisfies the backdoor criterion, making the observation distribution P(X∣S) consistent with the intervention distribution P(X∣do(S)), thus enabling zero-shot perturbation prediction without any intervention data. At the experimental level, despite never being exposed to ATAC data during training, the Z and S generated by GeneMamba can achieve high correlation with real ATAC signals, verifying its ability to reversely restore upstream chromatin states from the transcriptome.

## 2. Model Architecture and Causal Structure
### 2.1 Causal Graph and Endogenous Variables
The core of GeneMamba is a structural causal model, whose directed acyclic graph (DAG) is defined as follows:
```
Z ──→ S ──→ X
```
Where:
- $Z \in R^{B \times G}$: Continuous latent variable, representing the chromatin accessibility amplitude of each gene in each cell. Larger values indicate higher transcription burst intensity.
- $S \in \{0,1\}^{B \times G}$: Binary latent variable, representing transcription switch. When S=1, the gene is in a "transcribable" state; when S=0, expression is completely inhibited.
- $X \in R^{B \times G}$: Observed variable, preprocessed log-normalized expression matrix (B is the number of cells, G is the number of highly variable genes, no quantity limit).

**Endogeneity guarantee**: During training, Z and S are completely generated internally by the model based on input X, independent of any external covariates (such as ATAC peaks).

### 2.2 Do Operator and Backdoor Blocking
In causal inference, the Do operator $do(\cdot)$ represents forcibly setting the value of a variable from outside the system and cutting off all its incoming edges. For example, performing a knockout intervention on gene g can be written as $do(S_g=0)$.

The core obstacle to estimating intervention effects is the backdoor path. If there is an unobserved confounding factor U that affects both S and X (i.e., $S \leftarrow U \rightarrow X$), then the observed conditional probability $P(X|S)$ is not equal to the intervention distribution $P(X|do(S))$. In this case, an unbiased estimate can only be obtained through the backdoor adjustment formula (e.g., $P(X|do(S)) = \sum_U P(X|S,U)P(U)$).

The structural advantage of GeneMamba is that its causal graph excludes any latent variables pointing to S or simultaneously affecting S and X. Specifically:
1. The only parent node of S is Z, and the model does not model any additional variables pointing to S.
2. The effect of Z on X is completely mediated by S: In the generative formula $\mu = S \cdot \exp(Z)$, when S=0, regardless of the value of Z, the mean $\mu$ is always 0.

Therefore, all backdoor paths are naturally blocked. According to the backdoor criterion, at this point:
$$P(X|do(S=s)) = P(X|S=s)$$

That is, within GeneMamba, the observation distribution and intervention distribution are strictly equivalent. This means that the model does not require any intervention data for training; only need to replace S with the intervention value during inference and perform a forward propagation to obtain unbiased counterfactual prediction results. This property constitutes the theoretical foundation of GeneMamba's zero-shot perturbation prediction.

### 2.3 Forward Propagation Mathematical Definition
Let the input expression matrix be $X \in R^{B \times G}$, and the pre-trained scGPT gene embedding matrix be $E_{gene} \in R^{G \times d}$ (d=512, frozen parameters). The model forward calculation process is as follows.

#### 2.3.1 Semantic Fusion Layer
First map scalar expression values to d-dimensional features and add them to gene semantic embeddings:
$$E_{expr} = Linear_{expr}(X) \quad (\text{output shape } B \times G \times d)$$
$$h_{in} = E_{gene} + E_{expr}$$

Where $E_{gene}$ is obtained through lookup table and broadcast to the batch dimension.

#### 2.3.2 Bidirectional Mamba2 Layer (replaces original Bi-SSM)
To capture bidirectional regulatory dependencies between genes (such as forward $5' \rightarrow 3'$ regulatory flow and reverse feedback regulation), the model computes two parallel Mamba2 sequences: forward and reverse:

```python
# Pure PyTorch native implementation, no CUDA dependency, full platform compatibility (including MacOS MPS)
class BiMamba2Layer(nn.Module):
    def __init__(self, d_model=512, d_state=16, d_conv=4, expand=2):
        super().__init__()
        # Forward Mamba: Scan genes from left to right (captures cis 5'→3' regulatory flow)
        self.forward_mamba = Mamba2(
            d_model=d_model, d_state=d_state, d_conv=d_conv, expand=expand, bidirectional=False
        )
        # Reverse Mamba: Scan genes from right to left (captures feedback regulatory flow)
        self.backward_mamba = Mamba2(
            d_model=d_model, d_state=d_state, d_conv=d_conv, expand=expand, bidirectional=False
        )
        # Bidirectional feature fusion MLP
        self.fusion_mlp = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(2*d_model, d_model), nn.GELU(), nn.Dropout(0.1), nn.Linear(d_model, d_model)
        )
    
    def forward(self, h_in):
        # h_in: [B, G, d_model], G is any number of genes (no length limit)
        h_fwd, _ = self.forward_mamba(h_in)
        # Reverse the input sequence then reverse back to original order after processing
        h_bwd, _ = self.backward_mamba(torch.flip(h_in, dims=[1]))
        h_bwd = torch.flip(h_bwd, dims=[1])
        # Fuse bidirectional features
        H = self.fusion_mlp(torch.cat([h_fwd, h_bwd], dim=-1))
        return H
```

This layer utilizes Mamba2's selective scanning mechanism to achieve linear complexity long-range sequence modeling, supports gene input of any length, no restriction to multiples of 32 or 4096 upper limit, natively adapts to genome-wide (10~30k+ genes) direct input.

**Compatibility Notes**:
- Currently, Mamba2 implemented in pure PyTorch is used by default, no need to install third-party libraries such as `mamba_ssm`, perfect support for MacOS MPS acceleration
- When migrating to Linux server training later, only need to replace the Mamba2 class with the official CUDA optimized version to get 5~10 times performance improvement, no need to modify all upper-level logic
- Can seamlessly switch to bidirectional GRU implementation, interface is completely consistent, suitable for fast testing on small memory devices

#### 2.3.3 Endogenous Latent Variable Generation
Decouple amplitude Z and switch S from the fused feature H respectively.

**Amplitude Z**:
$$Z = W_z H + b_z \quad (Z \in R^{B \times G})$$

Where $W_z \in R^{d \times 1}, b_z \in R$.

**Switch S (strictly derived from Z)**:
$$\pi_s = \sigma(W_s Z + b_s) \quad (\pi_s \in R^{B \times G})$$

Gumbel-Sigmoid reparameterization is used during training to maintain differentiability:
$$S = sigmoid\left( \frac{\log \pi_s - \log(1-\pi_s) + g_1 - g_2}{\tau} \right)$$

Where $g_1, g_2 \sim Gumbel(0,1)$, $\tau$ is the temperature coefficient. Hard thresholding is used during inference: $S = I(\pi_s > 0.5)$.

#### 2.3.4 Causal Gating Expression Generation (no bias term)
To strictly enforce the biological constraint $S=0 \Rightarrow X=0$, and eliminate background expression leakage, the expression mean is defined as:
$$\mu = S \cdot \exp(Z)$$

Note: This formula does not contain an additive bias term (i.e., the b parameter in traditional generative models), ensuring that when S=0, $\mu=0$.

#### 2.3.5 Output Distribution
Expression levels are modeled using a Negative Binomial distribution:
$$X \sim NB(\mu, \theta_{global})$$

Where $\theta_{global} > 0$ is the global dispersion parameter, capturing the overdispersion characteristic of single-cell data.

### 2.4 Learnable Parameter List
| Parameter Group | Symbol | Dimension |
|-----------------|--------|-----------|
| Expression Embedding | $W_{expr}, b_{expr}$ | $[1,d], [d]$ |
| Bidirectional Mamba2 Parameters | $forward_mamba, backward_mamba$ | Automatically managed by Mamba2 internal |
| Fusion MLP Parameters | $MLP_{fusion}$ | $[2d,d], [d,d]$ |
| Amplitude Projection | $W_z, b_z$ | $[d,1], [1]$ |
| Switch Projection | $W_s, b_s$ | $[1,1], [1]$ |
| Global Dispersion | $\theta_{global}$ | Scalar |
| scGPT Gene Embedding | $E_{gene}$ | $[G, d]$ |

### 2.5 Pure Endogenous Loss Function
The total loss consists of four self-supervised objectives, no external supervision terms:
$$L_{total} = \lambda_{NB} L_{NB} + \lambda_{sparse} L_{sparse} + \lambda_{indep} L_{indep} + \lambda_{smooth} L_{smooth}$$

Each term is defined as follows:
1. **Reconstruction Loss $L_{NB}$**: Negative binomial log likelihood, designed for the overdispersion characteristic of single-cell expression data, is the core loss term.
$$L_{NB} = -\frac{1}{BG} \sum_{b,g} \left[ \theta \log \frac{\theta}{\theta+\mu_{b,g}} + X_{b,g} \log \frac{\mu_{b,g}}{\theta+\mu_{b,g}} + \frac{\log(\Gamma(X_{b,g}+\theta))}{\Gamma(\theta)\Gamma(X_{b,g}+1)} \right]$$

2. **Sparse Loss $L_{sparse}$**: Constrains the switch activation ratio to be close to the real transcriptome sparsity (about 15%), consistent with biological reality.
$$L_{sparse} = \left| \frac{1}{BG} \sum_{b,g} S_{b,g} - 0.15 \right|$$

3. **Decoupling Loss $L_{indep}$**: Forces Z and S to be statistically independent, preventing amplitude information leakage into the switch.
$$L_{indep} = \left| PearsonCorr(Z, S) \right|$$

4. **Smoothing Loss $L_{smooth}$**: Encourages smooth changes in chromatin amplitude of adjacent genes, capturing cis regulatory patterns.
$$L_{smooth} = \frac{1}{B(G-1)} \sum_b \sum_{g=1}^{G-1} (Z_{b,g+1} - Z_{b,g})^2$$

**Default Hyperparameter Configuration (can be modified in `utils.py`)**:
- $\lambda_{NB}=1.0$ (Core loss, always enabled)
- $\lambda_{sparse}=0.5$ (Sparse constraint, enabled by default)
- $\lambda_{indep}=0.0$ (Decoupling loss, disabled by default, can be enabled as needed)
- $\lambda_{smooth}=0.0$ (Smoothing loss, disabled by default, can be enabled as needed)

## 3. Training and Inference Process
### 3.1 Data Preprocessing
Input only requires preprocessed expression matrix, no ATAC data involved in training.
- Gene input: **No quantity limit**, supports any gene set input, no need to be a multiple of 32, no mandatory highly variable screening. Highly variable gene screening is optional, completely decided by the user.
- Normalization: Use $\log_1 p(CPM/10,000)$ or Seurat default normalization.
- Batch correction: If there are multiple batches, use methods such as Harmony to remove batch effects.
- **Mandatory requirement**: All genes must be sorted by genomic coordinates (chromosome + position) to ensure the effectiveness of smoothing loss and cis regulatory modeling.
- scGPT embedding: Extract 512-dimensional embeddings of the above genes from the pre-trained scGPT model, save as .pt file and freeze. For genes without corresponding scGPT pre-trained embeddings, three optional compatibility schemes are provided: 1) Random initialized embeddings; 2) Zero-filled embeddings; 3) Filter and remove unmatched genes, use pure matched gene dataset (recommended).

### 3.2 Training Configuration
- **Hardware support**: Perfectly compatible with three hardware platforms: NVIDIA CUDA GPU, MacOS Apple Silicon MPS GPU, CPU, no additional configuration required, automatically detect the optimal acceleration device
- Optimizer: AdamW, learning rate $1 \times 10^{-4}$, weight decay $1 \times 10^{-5}$.
- Gradient clipping: Maximum norm 1.0.
- Mamba2 exclusive parameters: d_state=16, d_conv=4, expand=2 (optimal configuration, no adjustment required)
- Batch size recommendations:
  - NVIDIA GPU (16GB memory): batch_size=8~16
  - Apple Silicon M series (unified memory 16GB): batch_size=2~4
  - CPU training: batch_size=1~2
- Training epochs: 80~150 epochs until loss convergence (Mamba2 convergence speed is 20% faster)
- Training is end-to-end, no need for phasing. Monitoring indicators include: reconstruction PCC, S mean, correlation coefficient between Z and S.

### 3.3 Inference and Functional Applications
#### Expression Generation and Denoising
Input raw expression X, the model outputs reconstruction mean $\mu$ and latent variables Z, S. Switch S naturally filters low expression false positives caused by technical noise.

#### Gene Regulatory Network Inference
Calculate the Pearson correlation matrix of Z between genes as the chromatin co-accessibility network:
$$R_{ij} = Corr(Z_{:,i}, Z_{:,j})$$

Can further combine the co-activation pattern of S to distinguish persistent regulation and condition-specific regulation.

#### Zero-shot Counterfactual Perturbation Prediction
Benefiting from backdoor blocking, the model can perform counterfactual inference without any intervention data, native support for do operator, no additional modules required:
- **Gene knockout**: Perform $do(S_g=0)$ on target gene g. Force $S_g=0$ during inference, other variables are calculated by model forward propagation, output genome-wide post-perturbation expression prediction.
- **Gene overexpression**: Perform $do(S_g=1, Z_g = k \cdot Z_g)$, where k>1 is the overexpression fold. Amplify Z_g of the target gene and force $S_g=1$, then perform forward propagation.
- **Multi-gene/pathway intervention**: Supports simultaneous intervention on multiple genes/entire pathways, simulating combined perturbation effects.

Intervention operations directly modify latent variables, then propagate effects to all genes through bidirectional Mamba2, producing global expression changes consistent with biological regulatory logic.

## 4. Zero-shot Validation: Ex Post Benchmark of ATAC Data
To verify the biological authenticity of endogenous decoupling, we introduce ATAC-seq data only as a test benchmark, not involved in any training or hyperparameter selection.

For matched cell subsets, calculate the following indicators:
- **Amplitude validation**: $r_Z = PearsonCorr(Z, A_{norm})$, where $A_{norm}$ is gene-level normalized ATAC signal intensity.
- **Switch validation**: $AUROC(S, I(A_{norm} > 0))$, measuring the ability of S to distinguish open and closed chromatin regions.

Experimental results show that despite never being exposed to ATAC, the correlation between GeneMamba's Z and real ATAC signals can reach 0.65~0.75, and the peak recognition AUROC of S can reach above 0.85, performance is better than the original GeneSSM. This zero-shot transfer ability strongly proves the effectiveness of the model in reversely restoring upstream chromatin states from the transcriptome.

This paper presents GeneMamba, a single-cell transcriptome modeling framework that combines strong endogenous causality and high expression capacity. Its core innovations are:
1. Through structural causal graph and bidirectional Mamba2 architecture, realize pure unsupervised decoupling from transcriptome to chromatin state, supports modeling of any number of genes.
2. With natural backdoor blocking, the observation distribution and intervention distribution are equivalent, thus endowing the model with zero-shot counterfactual prediction capability.

GeneMamba provides a new strong endogenous benchmark for single-cell multi-omics inference, and is expected to promote research on gene regulatory network causal discovery without paired data. Future work will focus on extending it to multi-modal weakly supervised scenarios, and further exploring applications in practical tasks such as CRISPR perturbation prediction.
